Why Base-12?

Base-10 is a counting system built around the number of fingers on two hands. It is useful for commerce and measurement. But it is not the system that nature uses to organise cycles. The clock runs on 12. The musical octave divides into 12 semitones. The zodiac organises into 12 positions. The human body has 12 cranial nerves, 12 thoracic vertebrae, 12 ribs. The Sumerian and Babylonian systems — the oldest mathematical traditions we have record of — were base-60, which is a multiple of 12.

When I began mapping the recurring patterns in my own lineage, I found that they organised naturally into 12 positions. Not 10. Not 8. Twelve. Each position corresponded to a distinct quality of experience: a specific way that fear, grief, anger, or disconnection expressed itself in the body. I did not impose this structure. I found it already there, waiting to be named.

Why 16 Frequencies?

The audio stack architecture in this system uses four layers: Foundation, Breath (mid-low), Mid, and Binaural/High. Each layer requires a minimum of four frequencies to create what I call a “coherent field” — a stack where the harmonic relationships between tones reinforce rather than cancel each other. Four layers times four frequencies equals sixteen. This is the minimum viable stack for a complete session.

The 16 core frequencies in the system are drawn from three sources: the Solfeggio scale (which divides into 9 tones, all multiples of 3), the Schumann resonances (Earth's electromagnetic field harmonics, beginning at 7.83 Hz), and the 432 Hz tuning system (where A4 = 432 Hz rather than the modern 440 Hz standard). These three sources share a common mathematical ancestor: they are all expressible as multiples or harmonics of 3, 6, 9, and 12.

Amplitude Scaling

If all 16 harmonics were generated at equal volume, the result would be acoustically harsh — a dense, buzzing tone with no sense of depth or ground. To create a meditative, layered timbre, each harmonic's amplitude is scaled inversely to its position in the series. The formula used is 1.0 / (n ^ 0.6) where n is the harmonic number. This means the fundamental (n=1) plays at full volume, while each successive harmonic is progressively quieter — present, but receding into the upper registers as shimmer rather than dominance.

The 7.83 Hz Schumann Resonance pulse is added as a binaural element beneath the harmonic stack. This is the fundamental electromagnetic resonance of the Earth's atmosphere — the frequency at which the space between the Earth's surface and the ionosphere resonates. Its inclusion is intended to entrain the listener toward the Alpha/Theta brainwave boundary (7–12 Hz), the state associated with deep relaxation, meditative awareness, and open-field perception.
